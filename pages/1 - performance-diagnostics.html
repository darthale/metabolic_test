The maximal oxygen uptake capacity (VO2max) is both a metabolic and respiratory as well as a muscular value:

It shows on one hand how much oxygen in milliliters per minute (absolute value) as well as in mL per minute per kilogram body weight (relative value) can be absorbed by the body.

Based on the development of the absolute VO2max over longer periods, it can be determined whether the training has led to muscular development, which increases the potential capacity, or whether the body is in a catabolic state in which muscle proteins are actively metabolized under stress.
