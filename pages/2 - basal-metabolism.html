The breathing pattern (respiratory volume and -frequency) can show whether the body can generally recover well (= calm, deep breathing) or is under stress (= high respiratory frequency and low respiratory volume).

The basal metabolic rate BMR (Basic Metabolic Rate) shows the total energy requirement in kilocalories on an average active day. The resting values are later calculated based on the measured individual, metabolic equivalent iMET. The measured values for oxygen uptake (VO2) and release of CO2 (VCO2) provide information about how efficiently the body cells process oxygen outside of athletic stress and whether the body is possibly under stress. Here, the Respiratory Quotient (RQ) serves as a reference point: if it is between a value of 0.8 and 0.9, the assumption is justified that the body is healthy and can function stress-free outside of exercise.
