Physiological and Metabolic Training Zones:
The difference between physiological and metabolic training zones and those calculated according to a specific method is that the zones and intensities listed here are based on actually measured performances and physical reactions.
In calculated zones, a threshold value is usually taken as a 100% mark, from which the zones are then calculated. Since this does not take into account the important aspects of stress such as oxygen uptake and utilization, metabolism and cardiovascular reactions, there is a high risk that work with it will cause long-term damage to the body. The areas presented here also include the individual data of substrate kinetics (dynamic energy balance) visualized on the following page and are defined as follows:
Zone 1:
Active Recovery
Low intensity, in which the body can use the absorbed oxygen to actively reduce stressors.
Zone 2:
Muscular Base
Intensity at which no stress is triggered at the muscular level. This area ends at VT1.
Zone 3:
Development Zone
In this zone, the body has to work more intensively with all systems. Too much movement in this zone can lead to overtraining.
Zone 4:
Threshold Zone
Intensity that trains the balance between oxygen uptake and deficit at the respiratory level.
Zone 5:
Aerobic Maximum
Exhaustion and development of the muscular oxygen uptake potential.
Zone 6:
Maximum Zone
Pure neuromuscular work: In this zone, no metabolic processes are triggered anymore.
Zone M1:
FatMax
Zone of maximum fat burning (by quantity). The end of this zone marks the beginning of possible metabolic stress.
Zone M2:
CarbMax
Zone in which the body burns between 60 and 90 grams of carbohydrates per hour.
